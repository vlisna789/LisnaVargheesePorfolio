Deloitte Client Work

Mostly Classified...

Deloitte work is a secret I will take to the grave, so I can't reveal too much, but I do want to touch on my impact and the type of work I have been able to create. I’ve had the opportunity to design for Fortune 500 clientele and work with the most amazing creators.


My Impact

I have worked with a diverse set of topics, ranging from life science to drone experts. I have led in the definition and creation of detailed dashboards and style guides, assisting in high intensity challenging work. Some of my proudest moments are listed below.


1. Platform Redesign

I had the opportunity to assist in Deloitte’s most powerful Life Science platform redesign. This included redefining everything from reorganizing the information architecture to developing a brand guide for their visualization library. To this day, Deloitte uses this work as an example of “Best in Class” work to their Life Science clients.


2. Financial Corporation Dashboards

I assisted in the definition and creation of a dashboard for a finance corporation. The dashboards were designed for 4 levels of risk management, from analyst to C suite executives. I aided in 10+ user interviews, creation of user flows, personas and 4 distinct dashboards. By end of the project, the client recognized the value of the design process and extended the work.


3. State of Clinical Research

I aided in the creation of a clinical research portal for a global organization. We developed a centralized source of truth that integrates the analytics portfolio of a research facility into a visually engaging, intuitive and easy to navigate interface. The interface included intense detail to microinteraction and branding as well.


Personal Takeaways

I love my work at Deloitte as a designer and I have been able to learn so much within these past years.

  • Asking Questions. It can be intimidating being the newest and youngest on a team but my perspective is vital to the movement of the product and pushing the outcome. I have learned to be unafraid of asking questions and poking holes.
  • Reading the Room. Consultant work is people work, learning to understand body language and voice fluctuations is a necessary role. With the onset of the pandemic and online meetings, the skill has been harder to develop but I have learned to love to read the silences and the awkwardness for the sake of a successful project.
  • Design Together. Being a UX designer means designing with others. I'm highly reliant on other people's expertise and subject knowledge. To resolve that, I'm learning to facilitate more conversations, and understand more nuance.
