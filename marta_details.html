MARTA Kiosk Redesign

This is a personal project I took on, where I choose a system I was innately familiar with, conducted a survey, performed an audit and performed a competitive analysis of other similar systems. In conclusion, based on my research, created a whole new flow.

Introduction to Marta

MARTA stands for Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority. MARTA is Atlanta’s main transit system.

If you are regular MARTA user, you would purchase a breeze card and reload your breeze card with a MARTA kiosk. The breeze card grants access to the ...

  • MARTA’s buses and trains
  • Cobb County Transit System
  • Gwinnett County System
  • GTRA

Because of all the access the MARTA card has - the kiosk has a lot of rules and flows.

Challenges

While designing this system I kept in mind two of my biggest limitations, the physical buttons and the various access options the breeze card gave.

Due to the fact that there are so many opportunities for your breeze card, the kiosk can be overwhelming. Not only are there too many options, but there is no priority for commonly used user actions , such as those who own a breeze card.

Because the physical buttons were placed evenly on the screen and all equidistant from one other, I had no free choice over where to place the options.

User Research

I created a short 10 question survey about users’ experience with the MARTA system. From that survey I got 34 response and most of the responses were 18-35 years old.

In short, the results are

  • 93% of users surveyed use the MARTA trains
  • 83% of users surveyed use the kiosk to reload their card
  • 70% of users surveyed buy either 2 trips or more trips
  • 60% of users surveyed complain that the kiosk lacks efficiency
  • 62% of users surveyed wish they could view the bus schedules on the kiosk

Personal Takeaways

  1. DESIGN FOR SCALE. The reality is most of the people taking my survey did not take the marta regularly, so making sure my design was customizable and flexible for all users.
  2. Data Driven Design. I have my own opinions of MARTA because I use the machine a lot, so taking my opinion out of problem and evaluating the results.
  3. Survey Design. Survey design requires a lot of mental work. I learned how to consider what ane enjoyable, clear and short survey would be.
  4. Working around business requirements. I knew MARTA's constraints were due to its complex bus and train rules for card usage and the fact the machine had 10 buttons total for interaction. Considering those limitations made this project challenging in the best ways.
  5. Audit. I learned how to audit a system. From flow, to usabilty features to copy, I took apart this machine.
